# ADR-003: Invite-Only Pilot and Transparent Usage Charging

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-15

## Context

The first HouseSearch release is an assisted pilot for independent brokers.
Usage is sold as confirmed search cases, but the product must define when a unit
is consumed, how refinements behave, what happens when allowance is exhausted,
and whether a technical failure should be billed.

## Decision

Administrators invite and activate pilot brokers; public registration is
outside the MVP. The assisted trial lasts 14 days and includes 10 search cases.
The Founding Plan costs BRL 149 per month and includes 30 search cases. Billing
and payment reconciliation remain manual.

A unit is consumed only when the broker confirms criteria and opens a search
case. The broker may explicitly refine that case with any confirmed criteria
changes for seven days without another unit. After expiry, further work requires
a new billable case.

If platform or approved-source failures leave a case with zero verified
properties, HouseSearch automatically restores its unit. A legitimate no-match
after successful research and a partial result with verified properties remain
billable.

Trial users who exhaust their allowance must activate the paid plan. Paid users
who exhaust their monthly allowance must explicitly accept a BRL 5 overage
before each additional case opens.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Automatic overages

- **Description**: Open every additional paid-plan case and reconcile all
  overages at month end.
- **Pros**: Removes interruption from the search flow.
- **Cons**: Creates surprise charges during a manually billed pilot.
- **Why rejected**: Explicit consent is more important than one fewer click.

### Alternative 2: Charge only for complete Top 3 results

- **Description**: Consume a unit only after three broker-approved options are
  delivered.
- **Pros**: Closely ties payment to the headline outcome.
- **Cons**: Makes successful research with sparse legitimate inventory free and
  creates incentives to reject results.
- **Why rejected**: A confirmed research case is the purchased unit; only a
  zero-result technical failure warrants a credit.

### Alternative 3: Infer whether a refinement is materially new

- **Description**: Have HouseSearch decide whether criteria changes constitute
  a new billable request.
- **Pros**: Could limit broad reuse of one case.
- **Cons**: Produces opaque, disputable billing decisions.
- **Why rejected**: A simple seven-day window chosen explicitly by the broker is
  predictable and auditable.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Brokers know the cost before every billable action.
- Refinement supports the natural buyer conversation without metering messages.
- Complete technical failures do not consume paid value.
- Invite-only onboarding supports hands-on pilot assistance.

### Negative

- Manual payment and account activation require administrator work.
- Overage confirmation adds friction after allowance exhaustion.
- Broad changes remain free when the broker deliberately uses the same case
  within seven days.

### Risks

- Failure attribution could be disputed; the product must distinguish a valid
  empty market result from zero verified data caused by system or source errors.
- Repeated retries must not consume or restore the same unit more than once.

## Implementation Notes

The TechSpec must make usage consumption, crediting, and overage acceptance
idempotent and auditable without changing the user-visible rules above.
